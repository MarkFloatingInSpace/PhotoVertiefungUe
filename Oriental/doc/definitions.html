Coordinate Systems¶

OrientAL generally uses 3-dimensional, right-handed cartesian coordinate systems.

Digital Image Coordinate System¶

For point coordinates in digital images, OrientAL generally (and especially MonoScope) defines the image and camera coordinate systems to be identical, with their origin being in the center of the upper/left pixel of an image, the \(x\)-axis being row-aligned, pointing to the right (in the direction of increasing column indices), and the \(y\)-axis being column-aligned, pointing upwards (in the direction of decreasing column indices). Thus, \(y\)-coordinates of image coordinates are generally negative:

Units¶

As planar angular unit, OrientAL generally uses the gon (gradian, grad), where a full circle is divided into 400 angular units. Apart from that, OrientAL has no notion of units, specifically not of units of length. OrientAL merely assumes all axes of its 3-dimensional, cartesian coordinate systems to have the same scale i.e. the same unit.

Spatial Similarity Transformation¶

OrientAL generally follows resp. ORIENT-definitions: all observations are modelled with the three-dimensional spatial similarity transformation, or a specialization thereof:

\[( \boldsymbol{p} - \boldsymbol{p_0} ) = s \cdot \boldsymbol{R}^T \cdot ( \boldsymbol{P} - \boldsymbol{P_0} )\]

where \(\boldsymbol{R}^T\) means \(\boldsymbol{R}\) transposed and

  • \(\boldsymbol{p}\) is the observed point in the local/sensor coordinate system,

  • \(\boldsymbol{p_0}\) is the point of rotation in the local/sensor coordinate system,

  • \(s\) is the model scale,

  • \(\boldsymbol{P}\) is the observed point in the superior coordinate system,

  • \(\boldsymbol{P_0}\) is the point of rotation in the superior coordinate system, and

  • \(\boldsymbol{R}\) is the \(3 \times 3\) rotation matrix,

All points mentioned above have 3 elements, which we generally denote with the 3 letters \(x\), \(y\), and \(z\), either in upper or lower case.

\(\boldsymbol{p_0}\) defines the interior orientation. \(\boldsymbol{R}\) and \(\boldsymbol{P_0}\) together define the exterior orientation

Perspective Transformation¶

For perspective image observations, \(\boldsymbol{p}\) is an image point, as observed e.g. with MonoScope. \(\boldsymbol{p_0}\) defines the interior orientation, with its elements named \(x_0\), \(y_0\), and \(c\). \(x_0\) and \(y_0\) together define the position of the principal point. \(c\) defines the focal length. \(\boldsymbol{P_0}\) is then the position of the camera’s projection centre in the superior coordinate system.

Due to perspective projection, the third dimension of object space gets lost. Defining all image points to lie in the image plane (\(z_i = 0 \,\forall i \in I\) with \(I\) being the image plane), this results in the image scale \(s\) being generally variable throughout an image. However, by transforming the formula of the Spatial Similarity Transformation, this unknown cancels out:

\[ \begin{align}\begin{aligned}\frac{x-x_0}{-c} = \frac { s \cdot [(X-X_0) \cdot r_{00} + (Y-Y_0) \cdot r_{10} + (Z-Z_0) \cdot r_{20} ]} { s \cdot [(X-X_0) \cdot r_{02} + (Y-Y_0) \cdot r_{12} + (Z-Z_0) \cdot r_{22}] }\\\frac{y-y_0}{-c} = \frac { s \cdot [(X-X_0) \cdot r_{01} + (Y-Y_0) \cdot r_{11} + (Z-Z_0) \cdot r_{21} ]} { s \cdot [(X-X_0) \cdot r_{02} + (Y-Y_0) \cdot r_{12} + (Z-Z_0) \cdot r_{22}] }\end{aligned}\end{align} \]

with \(r_{ij}\) being the elements of the rotation matrix \(\boldsymbol{R}\):

\[\begin{split}R = \begin{bmatrix} r_{00} & r_{01} & r_{02} \\ r_{10} & r_{11} & r_{12} \\ r_{20} & r_{21} & r_{22} \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Parametrization of Rotation Matrices¶

OrientAL supports 3 different ways how to parametrize an orientation matrix by 3 rotation angles:

\(\omega \varphi \kappa\)¶

OrientAL constant: EulerAngles.omfika

Order of rotations about axes:

  1. \(\boldsymbol{R}_Z(\kappa)\)

  2. \(\boldsymbol{R}_Y(\varphi)\)

  3. \(\boldsymbol{R}_X(\omega)\)

Thus:

\[\begin{split}\boldsymbol{R}_{\omega,\varphi,\kappa} &= \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \cos \omega & - \sin \omega \\ 0 & \sin \omega & \cos \omega \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \cos \varphi & 0 & \sin \varphi \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ - \sin \varphi & 0 & \cos \varphi \\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \cos \kappa & - \sin \kappa & 0 \\ \sin \kappa & \cos \kappa & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ \end{bmatrix} \\[10pt] &= \begin{bmatrix} \cos \varphi \cdot \cos \kappa & - \cos \varphi \cdot \sin \kappa & \sin \varphi \\ \cos \omega \cdot \sin \kappa + \sin \omega \cdot \sin \varphi \cdot \cos \kappa & \cos \omega \cdot \cos \kappa - \sin \omega \cdot \sin \varphi \cdot \sin \kappa & - \sin \omega \cdot \cos \varphi \\ \sin \omega \cdot \sin \kappa - \cos \omega \cdot \sin \varphi \cdot \cos \kappa & \sin \omega \cdot \cos \kappa + \cos \omega \cdot \sin \varphi \cdot \sin \kappa & \cos \omega \cdot \cos \varphi \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

ORIENT: ROTPAR TYPE(2)

\(\varphi \omega \kappa\)¶

OrientAL constant: EulerAngles.fiomka

Order of rotations about axes:

  1. \(\boldsymbol{R}_Z(\kappa)\)

  2. \(\boldsymbol{R}_X(\omega)\)

  3. \(\boldsymbol{R}_Y(\varphi)\)

Thus:

\[\begin{split}\boldsymbol{R}_{\varphi,\omega,\kappa} &= \begin{bmatrix} \cos \varphi & 0 & \sin \varphi \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ - \sin \varphi & 0 & \cos \varphi \\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \cos \omega & - \sin \omega \\ 0 & \sin \omega & \cos \omega \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \cos \kappa & - \sin \kappa & 0 \\ \sin \kappa & \cos \kappa & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ \end{bmatrix} \\[10pt] &= \begin{bmatrix} \cos \varphi \cdot \cos \kappa + \sin \varphi \cdot \sin \omega \cdot \sin \kappa & - \cos \varphi \cdot \sin \kappa + \sin \varphi \cdot \sin \omega \cdot \cos \kappa & \sin \varphi \cdot \cos \omega \\ \cos \omega \cdot \sin \kappa & \cos \omega \cdot \cos \kappa & - \sin \omega \\ - \sin \varphi \cdot \cos \kappa + \cos \varphi \cdot \sin \omega \cdot \sin \kappa & \sin \varphi \cdot \sin \kappa + \cos \varphi \cdot \sin \omega \cdot \cos \kappa & \cos \varphi \cdot \cos \omega \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

ORIENT: ROTPAR TYPE(3)

\(\alpha \zeta \kappa\)¶

OrientAL constant: EulerAngles.alzeka

Order of rotations about axes:

  1. \(\boldsymbol{R}_Z(\kappa)\)

  2. \(\boldsymbol{R}_Y(\zeta)\)

  3. \(\boldsymbol{R}_Z(\alpha)\)

Thus:

\[\begin{split}\boldsymbol{R}_{\alpha,\zeta,\kappa} &= \begin{bmatrix} \cos \alpha & - \sin \alpha & 0 \\ \sin \alpha & \cos \alpha & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \cos \zeta & 0 & \sin \zeta \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ - \sin \zeta & 0 & \cos \zeta \\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \cos \kappa & - \sin \kappa & 0 \\ \sin \kappa & \cos \kappa & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ \end{bmatrix} \\[10pt] &= \begin{bmatrix} \cos \alpha \cdot \cos \zeta \cdot \cos \kappa - \sin \alpha \cdot \sin \kappa & - \cos \alpha \cdot \cos \zeta \cdot \sin \kappa - \sin \alpha \cdot \cos \kappa & \cos \alpha \cdot \sin \zeta \\ \sin \alpha \cdot \cos \zeta \cdot \cos \kappa + \cos \alpha \cdot \sin \kappa & - \sin \alpha \cdot \cos \zeta \cdot \sin \kappa + \cos \alpha \cdot \cos \kappa & \sin \alpha \cdot \sin \zeta \\ - \sin \zeta \cdot \cos \kappa & \sin \zeta \cdot \sin \kappa & \cos \zeta \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

ORIENT: ROTPAR TYPE(4)
